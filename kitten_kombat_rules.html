Kitten Kombat

  • Overview
  • Indepth rules
  • Cards!

The rules for Kitten Kombat aren't too complex. At the beginning of the game shuffle all three decks. Each player starts out with 9 lives. Deal out 1-2 companion cards to each player. Then deal out two black cards and two orange cards to each player. Roll a D6 to decide play order.
Turn process is as follows:



Phases Action(s)
Draw: Draw 1 card from either deck. Before you draw you may also devour one companion.
Prep: During this phase, you may play any number of black cards and equip up to one of them.
Combat: Choose a player(or one of their perplexed companions) and roll a D6 for your attack!


Additional Rules

Draw & Prep

At the beginning of your turn before you draw, you may devour one of your companions to gain two lives. You may eat killed companions as well but they will only restore one life.

Combat

At any point during any players turn after they draw their card you may turn one of your companions sideways(henceforth referred to as "Perplex") to activate their ability.

To determine if you take away a life from a defending kitten, roll a D6. If you roll a four or great you've successfully taken one of its lives.

Weapons and armor affect the required dice roll for damage. Having a +2 weapon means you only need to roll a 2 to damage a kitten. However having a +2 armor means you need to roll a 6.

Anytime!

Once during each players turn you may play one trick card from your hand.

If at any point one of the decks runs out, shuffle the graveyard and it becomes the new deck. Once during each players turn you may play one trick card from your hand.
